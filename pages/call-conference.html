A universal visual identity for a national conference

Design Brief

After working with a team of 8 graphic design students to develop our own individual concepts at the University of California Davis, my work was chosen to represent the C.A.L.L. (communities, arts, lands, learning) Imagining America Conference, which will be hosted by UC Davis in October 2017. The C.A.L.L. Conference is focused on welcoming intersection through acceptance and understanding of differences united under a gathering for social change.

Goal

The three key words embodying the conference’s purpose: Intersection, Community, and Experience. To create a cohesive visual identity for the Imagining America C.A.L.L. Conference that would encourage engagement among all individuals of all skills residing within the Davis, Sacramento, and Bay area community.

UC Davis as a Host Institute

For the past 16 years the Imagining America Conference has been hosted by the University of Wisconsin located in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. However in 2017, the University of California, Davis has been marked as the conference’s new host. Given this change, the C.A.L.L. Conference required a new and innovative image to reflect the educational institution of UC Davis’ commitment to both the local and international community.

Initial Logo Concepts

After researching Imagining America and their previous conferences further, I began to create mind maps to gain an understanding of the qualities of the conference and what the logo and visual identity should represent. Through this process I also drew symbols to narrow down the aspects of the C.A.L.L. Conference logo.

While creating these symbols I continuously focused on how the audience may interpret them and how the symbols could be used to represent a place where anyone could feel welcome to participate by sharing their stories, experiences, and knowledge.

Logo Concept Development

Following many iterations of logo designs, I decided to focus the logo development on illustrating a voice wave or frequency. The aim of the design was to represent the C.A.L.L. Conference as a place for all individuals to share their voice in a playful and approachable way.

Finished Logos

For the final rendition of the logo, I chose to expand on the concept of the voicewave by utilizing morse code to create the wave pattern within the logo. Thus, resulting in the formation of three interchangeable logo designs, each of which use morse code and four designated colors to represent a different category of C.A.L.L. , including Arts + Lands, Learning, and Communities.

The use of the morse code voice wave and the emphasis on the “all” in C.A.L.L., also further highlights the goal of the conference to be accessible to all individuals from all walks of life.

Conference Graphic Patterns

The patterns are drawn from the morse code used within the three versions of the final logo to represent the four categories of C.A.L.L. These patterns can further be utilized to create a consistent visual identity for all conference marketing materials.

Conference Items

The visual identity for the conference can further be translated into conference items or materials, such as badges and tote bags.

Conference Posters

Following the creation of the logo designs, the conference poster also underwent a series of iterations before landing upon a final poster that suited the conference's mission. The final poster design also recognizes the importance of UC Davis as a host institute by utilizing an archived photograph of an agricultural course held at UC Davis.

The use of this image and the display of concise information on the conference further provides an approachable visual message towards the viewers.
